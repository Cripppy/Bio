Cancer & Tumor

Cancerous Cells vs Normal Cells

1

Cancer cells will appear very differently from normal cells: different size and different shape. 
Cancer cells that do not fit together nicely results to tumor.

Benign Tumor vs Malignant Tumor

2

Benign (not cancer) tumor cells grow only locally and cannot be spread by invasion or metastasis. 
Malignant cancer cells invade neighboring tissues, enter blood vessels,and metastasize to different sites.

Nondisjunction

Nondisjunction

3

It is a condition in which homologous chromosomes or sister chromatids fail to separate in meiosis.
Usually leads to: 
     Monosomy - 1 copy of a chromosome
     Trisomy - 3 copies of a chromosome.

Aneuploidy of Autosomes

Down Syndrome/Trisomy 21 

3.1.1

The most common single cause of congenital disorder in humans affecting 1/740 live births. Upward slanting eyes and open mouth with tongue protrusion. Simian crease in palm, mental retardation, congenital heart defects, shorter life span, etc are the characteristics of this disorder.

Edwards Syndrome/Trisomy 18

3.1.2

It is the second most common autosomal trisomy after trisomy 21. It occurs in 1/5000  live births that severely affect all organ systems. High mortality rate that is usually due to the presence of cardiac and renal malformations, feeding difficulties, sepsis, and apnea caused by defects in the central nervous system are some of the characteristics of this disease.

Patau Syndrome/Trisomy 13

3.1.3

It is a rare abnormality that affects 1/10000 live births. Severe mental and brain defects that lead to seizures, apnea, deafness, and eye abnormalities. Congenital heart disease is present on approximately 80% of the patients. Many of the symptoms of this disorder are not compatible with more than a few months of life. 

Aneuploidy of Sex Chromosomes

Turner Syndrome

3.2.1

It is the only known viable monosomy in humans affecting 1/2000 live female births (and 15% of spontaneous abortions).
Phenotypically female who are sterile and may have a short stature, webbed neck, immature sex organs.

Klinefelter Syndrome

3.2.2

It approximately affects 1 in 500-1000 males born with an extra X chromosome.About 40% of conceptions with this syndrome survive the fetal period. 
More likely to affect males. Mental retardation and hypogonadism but mortality rate is not significantly higher than healthy humans.

Jacobs Syndrome

3.2.3

It occurs in 1/1000 live births. 
Usually very tall and thin. Antisocial or behavioral problems and learning disabilities. Intelligence is usually normal.

Metafemale/Triple X

3.2.4

It affects 7.4-15.6/10000 female live births. The fetal death rate of triple X syndrome is not notably higher than that for conceptions with normal chromosomes.
Learning disabilities and unstable motor coordination. Wide spaced eyes, wide spaced nipples, or abnormally-sized head.
