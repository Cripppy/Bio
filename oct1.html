CELL CYCLE


Cell Cycle is a series of steps that includes cell growth, duplication of genetic material (DNA), and cell division usually resulting into two identical cells. The process of cell cycle occurs in phases or stages. For our convenience, we generally divide the cell cycle into two phases, the Interphase and the M phase.

The Interphase prepares the cell for cell division. In this phase, the cell grows and makes a copy of its genetic material (DNA). On the other hand, the M phase is the dividing phase of the cell cycle. After going through a series of steps, the cell first divides the nucleus into two sets followed by the division of the cytoplasm that generates two identical daughter cells. This process of cell division is called mitosis. Similarly, instead of once, a parent cell divides twice to give birth to four daughter cells each with half of the amount of genetic material compared to the parent cells. This type of division is called meiosis, the one useful in the production of gametes. 

But to tell you one thing, the two phases doesn't have the same time duration. For the most crucial and challenging tasks like division of nucleus followed by cytoplasm, the cell takes a lot of time to prepare for this steps rather the actual divisions. The Interphase lasts much longer compared to the M phase. You will be astonished to know  that the Interphase occupies more than 90% of the total time required for one cycle. And the remaining less than 10% of the time is for the M phase. So, all the important functions like growth, duplication of the genetic material, synthesis of the new compounds and organelles, all of this are carried out by the Interphase. It is believed that the cell takes up 24 hours to complete one cycle. However, this duration can vary from an organism to an organism, from one cell type to the other.

Later, at the end of the cycle, the new cells produced does the same exact process all over again. That's the reason why its called a cycle as it does not follow a linear pathway. This is just an overview of the two major phases of the cell cycle.

Cell Cycle & Mitosis

The cell cycle, or cell-division cycle, is the series of events that take place in a cell leading to duplication of its DNA (DNA replication) and division of cytoplasm and organelles to produce two daughter cells.
